Spring 2019
ECE 4012 Senior Design (Expo)

DIANA - Drone Inspection Assistant for Navigable Aircraft

Drone Inspection Assistant of Navigable Aircraft (DIANA) is a proof of concept system for inspection automation of aircrafts, buildings, and other infrastructures. Using DIANA to perform visual inspections ensures the safety of the inspectors as well as the efficiency of the inspection process. DIANA aids in conducting routine maintenance and inspections for concrete structures and infrastructure by reducing the time, cost, and risk from current practices. The drone with accompanying tablet will provide real-time results to the inspection-operator.
Most of the efforts of our team were allocated to training a neural network, automating flight path, and creating a fully integrated demonstratable system. Check out some of project results below.
We also attended and presented our project at the Spring 2019 Georgia Tech Capstone Design Expo, one of the largest student design expos in the U.S.

Spring 2019
CX 4365 Intro to Enterprise Computing

Interesting Updates

The motivation behind Interesting Updates is to cut out the time spent searching for articles a user is interested in and present them with those articles up front. Interesting Updates customizes feeds to a personal level by picking up on a user's interests by monitoring trends in searches and web history.
Users do not interfere with the data collection process that molds their news feed. Articles are then presented via speech or on screen. The users can also choose to ask for a summary of the article and an NLP text-rank algorithm will give them a brief summary.

Doombots

The goal of this team project is to design and program a modified AmigoBot using Assembly(MIPS) and Quartus II program to achieve the objective of detecting and locating objects in an arena.
This project included a technical report as well as a team presentation. (Both are attached below for reference.)
Our team struggled in achieving the goal but we learned a lot about problem-solving and team synergy along the way.
In the end, our robot did not completely achieve the goal of object location and detection. We only tagged half of the objects placed in the arena. We analyzed our errors after the demonstration and recorded ways that we could improve the project.
